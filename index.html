Picking the extraction LLM for knowledge-graph ingestion.

Knowledge-graph RAG systems (Microsoft GraphRAG, LightRAG, the ecosystem around them) extract entities and relations from source text at ingest time using an LLM. Eleven candidate models measured on one such system, same corpus, same chunking, same rubric. Gemini 2.5 Flash clears every retrieval cell at roughly 3 percent of Sonnet 4.6's ingest cost. The counter-intuitive part: it wins on breadth (2x more entities per document) while its relation-to-entity ratio is lower. Why breadth dominates density for graph-traversal retrieval, what local open-source can and cannot do, and a per-100-document cost model a delivery lead can take to a budget conversation.
