Frontier ships coding agents as products.
Applied AI ships governed systems.

The AI market splits three ways in 2026: research that proves a capability, consumer products that dramatise it, and enterprise systems that have to survive scale, tenancy, audit, schema change, and adversarial input. Applied AI is the middle band — the discipline of taking a research-grade or consumer-grade capability and converting it into an enterprise substrate with measured properties, named failure modes, and an adoption path. This site publishes the working drafts of that work.

· About this site

Why this site exists.

I publish these reports in the spirit of open source — research shared widely to help engineering teams make better enterprise-AI adoption decisions. None of it claims to be the only way, or the right way for everyone; each report is one practitioner's notes on a specific question, measured on a specific stack. AI moves fast enough that any of this could be stale next week; I commit to updating my thinking here on a best-effort basis as the picture sharpens. The series is specialised — applied AI, not AI in general. Take what's useful; the rest is honest about where it stops.
